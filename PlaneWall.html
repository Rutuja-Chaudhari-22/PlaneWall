Heat Conduction Through A Plane Wall

STEP 1: DEFINE TERMS

L=Thickness of plane Wall
A=Cross-Sectional Area
K=Thermal Conductivity
T1=Temperature at phase 1
T2=Temperature at phase 2

STEP 2: Apply general heat conduction equation in cartesian co-ordinates

δ2t / δx2 + δ2t / δy2 + δ2t / δz2 + qg / k = 1/α δt / δτ


STEP 3: Use Conditions

Steady State ----> δt/ δτ = 0

One-Dimensional ----> δ2t/ δy2 = δ2t/ δz2 = 0

No Heat Generation ----> qg/k = 0

∴ δ2t/δx2 = 0

OR

δ2t/δx2 = 0

Step 4 : Integrating the equation Twice

δt/δx = C1

t = C1 + C2

               ----1

Step 5 : Using Boundary conditions to find C1 & C2

At, x = 0, t = t1
We get, t1 = C2

At, x = L, t = t2
We get, C1 = t2 − t1/L 

             ----From 1

t = (t2 − t1/L)x + t1

Temperature Distribution across a wall is Linear.

Step 6 : Use Fourier's Law

Q = K A δt/δx

Find δt/δx

-----> δ/δx[(t2 − t1/L)x + t1]

δt/δx = t2 − t1/L

Q = − K A (t2 − t1/L)

= K(t1 − t2/L)

Step 7 : Define Thermal resistance to heat Conduction

(Rth)cond = L/K A

Q = t1 − t2/(L/K A)

Q = t1 − t2/(Rth)cond
